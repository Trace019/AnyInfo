Audio Media

Design Elements

Dialogue

Any sound that makes use of specific intonations to
convey messages from one individual to another.

SFX

Vocal or instrumental sounds combined to
produce a piece with harmony and form.

Music

Additional sounds outside of music and dialogue,
These are often used to give emphasis and setting.

Silence

Absence of audio or sound. These are often used to
give emphasis or set the pacing of audio media.

Audio Media

Design Principles

Mixing

Combination, balance, and control
of multiple sound elements.

Stereo IMG

Making use of left and right channels
to add depth to the sound.

Pace

Time control, editing, and order of events.

Transition

Techniques used to move from one
segment to the other.

Motion Media

Design Elements

Speed

The pacing of the movement can be based on additional
audio or the context of the scenario.

Motion

This is where objects move can reflect the progression
or moving forward of information transfer.

Direction

These are the triggers that signify the change
in direction of an object.

Timing

This can be objective which is measured in actual units of
time or subjective where it is psychological rather than
accurate to passage of time.
